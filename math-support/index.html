Chicken McNugget Theorem

Imagine you can only buy chicken nuggets in packs of, say, 3 and 5.

The Chicken McNugget Theorem helps you find the biggest number of nuggets you can't buy.

It turns out, the biggest number you can't get with 3 and 5 is 7. You can get 3, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, and anything bigger. But you can't get 1, 2, 4, or 7.
So what is the formula?

Well, if you can only combine two numbers, m and n, the biggest number you can't make is (m * n) - m - n.
